Overview

The emergence of multimodal foundation models has catalyzed a major shift in human-centered vision. While traditional systems often struggle with open-world human behavior, large-scale pretraining and cross-modal reasoning now offer new ways to build adaptable and resilient visual intelligence.

This workshop focuses on the transition from constrained benchmarks to in-the-wild deployment, where perception systems must deal with ambiguous human intent, adverse imaging conditions, heterogeneous sensors, and dynamic environments. We aim to bridge theory and practice by bringing together work on robust perception, grounded reasoning, and real-world human-centered applications.

Topics of Interest

We welcome submissions and discussions related to, but not limited to, the following themes:

  • MLLMs and foundation models for human-centric visual understanding
  • Low-light, nighttime, and adverse-condition perception
  • Activity analysis, intent understanding, and behavior reasoning
  • Fusion of RGB, infrared, thermal, event, depth, and LiDAR modalities
  • Domain generalization, transfer, and adaptation for human-centered tasks
  • Benchmarking, evaluation, reliability, and safety in real-world settings
  • Efficient learning under limited annotations or low-resource conditions
  • Applications in surveillance, healthcare, robotics, transportation, and assistive systems
