The 1st Workshop on Human-Centered Multimodal Intelligence in the Wild

A workshop on foundation models and beyond for human-centered multimodal perception, reasoning, and real-world deployment
Date: July 12, 2026
Location: Placeholder Conference Venue
Co-located with: Placeholder Conference

The Human-Centered Multimodal Intelligence in the Wild (HCMIW) workshop bridges the gap between theoretical multimodal foundation models and practical, human-aligned applications. We focus on the synergy between robust perception and sophisticated reasoning, aiming to redefine how vision systems interpret human presence across heterogeneous sensors and dynamic real-world contexts.

Overview

The emergence of multimodal foundation models has catalyzed a paradigm shift in human-centered vision. While traditional systems often struggle with the open-world complexity of human behavior, the integration of large-scale pre-training with cross-modal reasoning offers new paths toward adaptive intelligence.

This workshop focuses on the transition from constrained benchmarks to in-the-wild deployment, where perception systems must deal with ambiguous human intent, adverse imaging conditions, heterogeneous sensors, and dynamic environments. We aim to bridge theory and practice by bringing together work on robust perception, grounded reasoning, and real-world human-centered applications.

Topics of Interest

We welcome submissions and discussions related to, but not limited to, the following themes:

  • Foundation models for human-centric perception, understanding, and reasoning.
  • Multimodal learning for human behavior understanding, interaction analysis, and activity recognition.
  • Perception in adverse conditions, including low light, occlusion, motion blur, and sensor noise.
  • Cross-modal grounding across RGB, thermal, infrared, depth, event cameras, LiDAR, and audio.
  • Generalization, adaptation, robustness, and reliability in real-world human-centered systems.
  • Benchmarks, datasets, evaluation protocols, and deployment-oriented analysis for in-the-wild scenarios.
